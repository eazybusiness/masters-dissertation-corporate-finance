# PLANNING — Master's Dissertation in Corporate Finance

## Project Overview

The client needs end-to-end support for a master's dissertation in accounting and finance, with a corporate finance focus. The research question is not yet defined — scope includes question refinement, literature review, methodology design, data collection guidance, quantitative analysis (Excel/Stata), academic writing, referencing, and critical editing. Final deliverable: a fully formatted, submission-ready dissertation plus a reflective summary.

## Proposed Approach

### Phase 1: Research Question & Proposal
- Discuss the client's general idea and interests within corporate finance
- Conduct a preliminary literature scan to identify viable, original angles
- Narrow down to a specific, testable research question
- Draft a 2–3 page dissertation proposal (background, RQ, methodology outline)
- Identify data requirements early to avoid bottlenecks

### Phase 2: Literature Review
- Systematic search across SSRN, Google Scholar, JSTOR, ScienceDirect
- Organise sources thematically (not chronologically)
- Write a critical literature review (not just a summary) identifying gaps
- Produce annotated bibliography as working document
- Typical length: 4,000–6,000 words depending on university guidelines

### Phase 3: Methodology & Data
- Design research methodology (quantitative — regression, panel data, event study, etc.)
- Advise on data sources (Bloomberg, Orbis, Datastream, WRDS, or freely available alternatives)
- Assist with data collection, cleaning, and variable construction
- Document methodology chapter with justification for every design choice
- Prepare Stata/Excel analysis templates

### Phase 4: Analysis & Results
- Run quantitative analysis (OLS, fixed effects, robustness checks)
- Create publication-quality tables and figures
- Write results chapter with interpretation tied back to literature
- Conduct sensitivity/robustness analysis
- Flag any unexpected findings and discuss implications

### Phase 5: Writing, Editing & Submission
- Write remaining chapters (Introduction, Conclusion, Abstract)
- Ensure all chapters are internally consistent and follow university guidelines
- Format references (Harvard/APA/Chicago as required)
- Critical editing pass for academic register, clarity, and argument flow
- Prepare reflective summary (key decisions during research process)
- Final formatting: table of contents, list of figures/tables, appendices
